# ProductManagement

### Enterprise Data Platform for Legal Technology

<img width="975" height="881" alt="Screenshot 2026-01-20 at 11 28 30 AM" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/980c9701-7b86-4e7f-9bde-a069733b57e1" />

The unified profiles functioned as a single, trusted “golden record” per matter, combining operational, experiential, and external intelligence.  Core Profile Components  Matter identifiers: case name, docket number, jurisdiction, court.  Internal attributes: practice group, responsible partners, staffing model.  Financial context: utilization, historical activity indicators (where appropriate).  External Enrichment  Judges and courts associated with the matter.  Motion activity, procedural events, and outcomes.  Litigation history tied to the case and jurisdiction.  Normalization & Searchability  Standardized court and judge names (no duplicates or aliases).  Consistent taxonomies for matter type, jurisdiction, and legal domain.  Fully searchable and filterable across practices and use cases.  User Experience  Attorneys and business teams could:  Quickly assess firm experience in a jurisdiction or before a judge.  Use profiles for client pitches, RFPs, and strategy discussions.  Start with a pre-populated profile and optionally validate or enrich it with additional context.  Profiles refreshed daily, ensuring relevance without manual upkeep.
